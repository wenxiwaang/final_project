Travel.

Explore famous landmarks, from the Hollywood Walk of Fame to the sunlit piers of Santa Monica, and immerse yourself in a world of entertainment and art. Get ready to experience the thrill and glamour of the city that never sleeps, right here in LA.

Santa Monica Pier

Enjoy a walk down the Pier and experience the breathtaking views
amidst the sounds of crashing waves and the ocean breeze.
This iconic pier offers a variety of entertainment, dining,
and shopping experiences, making it a popular destination
for both tourists and locals alike.

Griffith Observatory

Perched atop the southern slopes of Mount Hollywood in Griffith
Park, the Griffith Observatory offers a wonderful view of the City
of Los Angeles. The observatory is not only a hub for astronomical
science but also a beloved spot for its scenic hiking trails and as a
cultural meeting point within the city.

The Huntington Library

The Huntington Library, Art Museum, and Botanical Gardens is a
acelebrated cultural and research center set amidst 120 acres of
landscaped gardens. This expansive estate houses extensive
collections of rare books, art, and manuscripts in exquisite
galleries. Visitors can explore diverse botanical environments
providing a tranquil retreat from the urban sprawl of Los Angeles.

Getty Center

The Getty Center is a striking architectural triumph that offers
panoramic views of the city and the Pacific Ocean. This renowned
cultural institution showcases a vast collection of artworks
spanning from medieval times to the present. The Getty Center also
features beautiful gardens and innovative architecture.

The Broad

The Broad Museum in Los Angeles is a contemporary art museum
located in downtown LA. Free to the public, The Broad offers
visitors immersive and innovative art experiences, including
temporary exhibitions and installations that inspire and challenge
perspectives.

Little Tokyo

Little Tokyo in Los Angeles is a vibrant cultural district that serves as
a hub for Japanese-American history and culture. This lively
neighborhood features a variety of authentic Japanese restaurants
unique shops, and cultural landmarks such as the Japanese
American National Museum.

Disneyland

Disneyland Park, the original theme park designed by Walt Disney
himself features two unique theme parks: Disneyland Park and
Disney California Adventure Park. Disney California Adventure
Park offers experiences not found in other Disney parks, such as
the immersive Cars Land and the recently added Avengers Campus,
which brings Marvel superheroes to life in an interactive setting.

Rodeo Drive, Beverly Hills

Rodeo Drive is one of the world's most exclusive shopping streets,
renowned for its luxury boutiques and designer fashion. Beyond
shopping, It is also famous for its stunning architecture and
frequent appearances in pop culture, making it a top destination
for visitors seeking a taste of the luxurious Beverly Hills lifestyle.

Grand Central Market

Grand Central Market offers a bustling culinary experience in a
vibrant, open-air setting. This food emporium brings together a
diverse array of vendors offering everything from gourmet cheeses
and exotic spices to artisanal coffee and street food from around the
world.

Melrose Trading Post

The Melrose Trading Post is a popular weekly flea market held
every Sunday at Fairfax High School in Los Angeles, known for its
eclectic mix of vintage goods, handmade crafts, and unique finds.

The Americana at Brand

The Americana at Brand is an upscale outdoor shopping, dining, and
entertainment complex that combines the luxury of a modern
shopping mall with the charm of a quaint town square.

Point Dume State Beach, Malibu

Point Dume Beach in Malibu offers stunning views from its
dramatic cliffs and provides a secluded sandy stretch ideal for
sunbathing, swimming, and surfing. Visitors can explore the scenic
trails leading to an overlook with a panoramic view of the Pacific
Ocean.
